The Phototransduction Cascade

Published January 13, 2026

The phototransduction cascade is what happens when light hits the retina (the back of the eye).

Steps:

  1. Light hits the Cis-retinal and converts it to the trans-retinal
  2. Rhodopsin changes shape
  3. Rhodopsin releases transducin
  4. Transducin binds to a phosphodiesterase
  5. Phosphodiesterase converts cGMP to GMP
  6. Na+ channels close because of low cGMP levels
  7. Rod cell becomes hyperpolarized, turning OFF

Mnemonic:

CRRap no more TP NauR

"Crap, no more toilet paper, noooo"

This is part of a bigger-picture pathway of light hitting a rod and sending a signal to the brain:

  1. Light hits the rod
  2. Rod turns Off
  3. Bipolar cell turns on
  4. Retinal Ganglion cell turns on
  5. Signal travels through the Optic nerve
  6. Signal arrives at the Brain
